Evolution of democratic rights

Two centuries ago, only a handful of the world's population had access to democratic political rights. Closed autocracies were the norm in the 19th century, but a different picture appeared in the 20th century.

The chart below shows the number of people living in a liberal democracy over time. In a liberal democracy, citizens have multi-party, meaningful, free, and fair elections. All citizens are equal before the law, and the legislative and court systems constrain the elected government's actions.
